Formula for RICE Style Prioritization Score

We define an AI prioritization score based on weighted criteria:

Priority Score Formula:

Priority Score = V Ã— A Ã— F Ã— D C Ã— T

Where:

  • V = Business Value (Revenue growth, cost savings, market positioning)
  • A = Strategic Alignment (Vision alignment, competitive advantage)
  • F = Feasibility (Development complexity, integration readiness)
  • D = Data Readiness (Data availability, quality)
  • C = Cost & Risk (Financial investment, regulatory concerns)
  • T = Time-to-Value (Deployment speed, measurable impact timeframe)

Scoring Breakdown (1-5 Scale):

  • Higher scores indicate greater feasibility, alignment, and value.
  • Lower cost & time increase prioritization.
  • Dividing by Cost & Time favors high-impact, low-effort AI initiatives.

# Load required libraries
library(ggplot2)

# Define prioritization criteria with feasibility and cost scores
eisenhower_data <- data.frame(
  Criteria = c("Business Value", "Strategic Alignment", "Technical Feasibility", 
               "Data Readiness", "Cost & Risk", "Time-to-Value"),
  Feasibility = c(5, 4, 3, 4, 2, 4),
  Cost = c(2, 3, 4, 2.5, 5, 1)
)

Eisenhower Matrix Summary:

This matrix categorizes AI initiatives based on Feasibility and Cost, assigning them to four quadrants:

  • Quick Wins (High Feasibility, Low Cost): Business Value and Time-to-Value fall here, meaning they should be prioritized for immediate action.
  • Strategic Investments (High Feasibility, High Cost): Strategic Alignment and Data Readiness are positioned hereâ€”worthy long-term investments, despite their higher cost.
  • Rethink (Low Feasibility, Low Cost): Technical Feasibility sits in this area, indicating the need for further refinement before implementation.
  • Avoid (Low Feasibility, High Cost): Cost & Risk lands here, suggesting it's too expensive and difficult to justify implementation.

This visualization is useful for balancing resources effectively and ensuring high-value, achievable initiatives get priority.

Using this correlation visualization, we can assess dependencies and make evidence-based prioritization decisions.
